About Me

I lead day to day IT services with a user first, outcome driven mindset, balancing stability, cost, and pace across multi country, multi site environments. Beyond traditional SLAs, I incorporate experience based measures (XLA) and open text sentiment to make service quality visible to both IT and business leaders. That visibility supports better decisions during seasonal peaks, improving tech adoption rate, collaborative project deliveries and technology rollouts.


My way of working is disciplined but practical: simple and visible incident, problem, change, and request flows; clear escalation and bridge routines for major incidents; a structured CAB and a living CSI backlog so improvements dont get lost. I apply automation only where it removes friction to cut repetitive handling, improve communications, and speed handoffs and I rely on telemetry/monitoring tools and metrics to act before users escalate. With vendors, I keep SLAs/XLAs clear, run QBRs with scorecards, and manage renewals to protect both service and cost.


I value teams that can perform consistently under pressure and honest about trade offs, so I coach for clear runbooks and shared playbooks, use simple KPIs and sentiment to guide improvement, and keep stakeholders engaged with transparent updates and service reviews. In practice, this means stabilizing services through peaks, running clean cutovers and hypercare, tightening reporting and executive readouts, streamlining vendor portfolios without service loss, and steadily improving resolution quality and user effort. The goal is IT that users can feel fewer escalations, faster resolution, and fewer surprises for the business.
